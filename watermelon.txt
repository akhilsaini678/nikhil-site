<h1 class="applesh"><strong> 2.Apples</strong></h1>
<h4 class="applestext">
 One of the most popular fruits, <u class="applesunder">apples</u> are chock-full of nutrition.<p>

They’re rich in both <u style="color:green">soluble</u> and <u style="color:green">insoluble fiber</u>, such as pectin, hemicellulose, and <br>cellulose.<br> These help you manage your blood sugar levels, promote good digestion, and support<br> gut and heart health .<p>

In addition, they’re a good source of  vitamin <u style="color:green">C</u> and plant polyphenols, which are<br> disease-fighting compounds found in plants. <p>In fact, consuming apples regularly may lower your risk of heart disease, stroke,<br> cancer, overweight, obesity, and neurological disorders.<p>

Note that most of the polyphenols in apples are located just below the skin, so be sure<br> to eat it to reap the greatest benefits.<p>
